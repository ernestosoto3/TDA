# Personas

The following personas represent realistic users within the Puerto Rican sports ecosystem. Each persona reflects real-world behaviors, motivations, and challenges observed in local sports fandom, amateur athletics, and event organization.

## Persona 1 : Alfredo Guzman, the Casual Fan & Follower

**Background:**  
Alfredo is a 20 year old Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Puerto Rico at Mayagüez. He is rarely seen without his favorite BSN team cap, a pair of worn-out sneakers, and a water bottle covered with stickers. He is constantly active in three different WhatsApp group chats arguing about basketball trades and game plays. He loves *Empanadillas de camarones* on weekends, follows BSN, Liga de Béisbol Superior Doble A,and never misses a high-stakes rivalry game. However, he gets constantly frustrated having to jump between obscure Facebook pages, Instagram stories, and outdated league websites just to figure out what time a game starts or where to stream it.

**Goals:**
* Quickly check live scores, game schedules, and broadcast/streaming details for local Puerto Rican leagues.
* Receive reliable notifications for game tip-offs, delays, or venue updates.
* Track standings and player statistics across multiple local sports in one centralized place.

**Frustrations:**
* Finding conflicting game times posted across different social media accounts.
* Missing live games due to last-minute venue changes or unannounced schedule shifts.
* Fragmented sports coverage and difficulty finding official stream links for local matchups.

**Why this persona matters:** Alfredo highlights the need for centralized scheduling, reliable real-time notifications, and intuitive media integration for local sports followers.

## Persona 2 : Valeria "Val" Colón, the Professional Athlete & Rising Talent

**Background:**  
Valeria is a 24 year old professional volleyball player currently competing in Puerto Rico’s Superior League (LVSF). Her entire life revolves around practices, physical training, and traveling to municipalities for matches, with her ultimate goal being to secure a professional contract in Europe or be scouted by international teams. She is a powerhouse on the court but feels stuck in a digital dead end. In Puerto Rico, there are very few platforms focused on scouting and showcasing new talent. Her agents rely mostly on old contacts, and game footage is scattered or poorly produced. Valeria feels frustrated that, despite her performance, her visibility outside the island is minimal. She needs a dedicated platform to centralize her match highlights and career stats for a professional, scout-ready profile.

**Goals:**
* Create a polished, central player profile featuring high-quality video highlights and validated career statistics.
* Increase global visibility and accessibility for international scouts, college recruiters, and agency pools.
* Track individual and team performance metrics in a professional, easily digestible format.

**Frustrations:**
* Lack of modern, localized sports platforms focused on scouting talent and professional recruitment.
* Game footage is fragmented across social media, making it hard to compile a consistent, impactful highlights reel.
* Difficulty getting eyes on her performance from scouts who are not physically present in Puerto Rico.

**Why this persona matters:** Valeria represents the vital need for a modern "scouting pipeline" within the app, providing a platform for professional and aspiring Puerto Rican athletes to showcase their specific highlights to a broader, global audience.

## Persona 3 : Emeterio Santiago, the Local League Administrator

**Background:**  
Emeterio is a 52 year old physical education teacher and youth league coordinator in Caguas. He is punctuality personified, always arriving 15 minutes early with a metal clipboard, a whistle around his neck, and a thermos of strong black coffee. He manages an 8 team adult basketball league and coordinates referees, court rentals, and official box scores. Currently, he runs the entire operation using Excel spreadsheets, paper scorecards, and endless WhatsApp messages. Rain delays and court maintenance frequently disrupt his calendars, forcing him to manually update and message dozens of players and team captains individually.

**Goals:**
* Efficiently create and manage season schedules, team rosters, and league standings.
* Quickly publish updated scores, stat sheets, and game rescheduling notices.
* Maintain clear, direct communication with team leaders, referees, and tournament followers.

**Frustrations:**
* Manually entering box scores and calculating league standings after late night games.
* Answering repetitive messages from players asking about game times or standings tables.
* Handling schedule changes and venue shifts caused by bad weather or court conflicts.

**Why this persona matters:** Emeterio highlights the necessity of robust administrative management tools, automated standings and stats calculation, and instant group announcement features.
